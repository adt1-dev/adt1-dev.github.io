Installing a custom recovery on your ADT-1

This is a work in progress...

To flash a custom recovery on your device, first, choose a recovery: TWRP (Reccomended) or CM Recovery (Not supported by this guide, only use if you know what you are doing)

TWRP = https://dl.twrp.me/molly/twrp-2.8.7.0-molly.img.html
CM Recovery = https://www.androidfilehost.com/?fid=24052804347773639

Install the newest ADB/Fastboot (Google it).

Now, reboot to bootloader (fastboot) mode.
Do this by unplugging the ADT-1, holding the button on the back, and plugging it back in.

Open a cmd/terminal and run:
fastboot flash recovery /path/to/recovery.img

Use the button on the back on the ADT-1 to highlight "Recovery Kernel", then long press the button to enter select it. You are now in TWRP.

NOTE: You will need a USB mouse plugged in to navigate, the remote does not function in recovery.


Installing our Unofficial CyanogenMod 12.1 Builds


WARNING: THIS DELETE YOUR APPS! Your SDCard's contents will remain intact.

NOTE: Many things are broken in these builds! Take a backup of /system (and if you want, your /data)in TWRP in case!

Don't report bugs that are already known, check the "Issues" tab on out GitHub (linked above). If it isn't there, first do your best to recreate the bug, and list the steps you took, then report it.

GApps have some odd bugs, we reccomend not using them right now, it can break bluetooth.

Please downlaod the newest build from here:
https://www.androidfilehost.com/?fid=24052804347772973

Optionally download the newest GApps from here:
https://www.androidfilehost.com/?fid=24052804347772987

Wipe /data, /system, /cache, and dalvik-cache

Flash the ROM (and optionally, GApps) via TWRP.
(you can drag and drop the file via USB while you are in TWRP)

Now, if you want to, flash SuperSU, though, its completely unnecessary, and reccomended against, as CM12.1 has optional Root baked into Developer Settings.


Known Issues


1. BT does not function if the GApps package is flashed -- Probably due to lack of the BT pairing tutorial APK, should be fixed in the next GApps
2. Video is somewhat choppy -- Probably a FrameBuffer issue, looking into it
3. HDMI Audio out does not work -- Configuration error, looking into it
4. AudioFX FC's -- related to Issue #3 -- see issue #3
5. The Sound Panel in LeanBack Settings Crashes and causes a Bootloop -- see issue #3
6. Some Videos (YouTube, Google Play Movies, etc.) won't play -- DRM Issue, No leads yet
7. DRM Issues -- No Leads Yet

Reverting to Stock


WARNING: THIS DELETE YOUR APPS! Your SDCard's contents will remain intact.

Download this file:
http://coggy.euroskank.com/LatestADTSystem.zip

Drag and drop the file via USB (Must be in TWRP).

Wipe /data, /system, /cache, and dalvik-cache

Install the .zip you downloaded.

Congrats, you are now on Stock 5.0.2 (LRX22G), if you want root, just flash SuperSU via TWRP.

Rooting Stock LRX22G

Follow the steps outlined in the "Installing a custom recovery on your ADT-1" section above.

Now, download SuperSU and flash via TWRP.
